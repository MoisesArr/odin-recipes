Honey BBQ Chicken Strip Sandwich

Description

The Honey BBQ Chicken Strip Sandwich is a mouthwatering delight that combines the perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors. This sandwich features crispy, golden-brown chicken strips that are generously coated in a rich and tangy honey BBQ sauce. Nestled inside a soft, toasted bun, the chicken strips are complemented by fresh, crunchy lettuce and a hint of creamy ranch dressing for an extra layer of flavor. Each bite offers a satisfying crunch followed by a burst of the smoky, sweet BBQ sauce, making this sandwich a favorite for BBQ lovers and anyone seeking a hearty, flavorful meal.

Prep Time: 10 Mins
Cook Time: 10 mins
Additional Time: 15 mins
Total Time: 20 mins
Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 8 SlicesTexas Toast
  • 3 tbsp Butter
  • 8 slices Monterey Jack Cheese
  • 4 tbsp honey barbecue sauce
  • 2 ½ cups Buttermilk
  • 1 ½ pounds chicken tenders
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 ½ teaspoons pepper
  • ½ teaspoon garlic salt
  • 2 ½ cups buttermilk
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Make the seasoned flour by combining flour, salt, pepper, and garlic salt. Stir the flour until it is well blended.

  2. Make a batter of the seasoned flour and buttermilk by pouring in about 1/2 cup of the buttermilk into the flour. You want the batter a little shaggy.

  3. Coat the chicken with the batter and place on a rack and allow it to dry. The batter may fall off when cooking if you don't allow the batter to try the chicken.

  4. Discard remaining buttermilk.

  5. Heat oil to 350 degrees.

  6. Gently place 3 or 4 breaded chicken tenders into the hot oil. Cook chicken tenders a few at a time until they are golden brown. Drain on a clean wire rack that has been placed on top of a baking sheet.

  7. Butter both sides of the Texas Toast. Cook the bread on a skillet or a griddle until the bread is golden brown, flip the bread over and brown the other side of the bread.

  8. Place three chicken strips on four slices of bread, then top with 2 slices of Monterey Jack Cheese, and then spread about 1 tablespoon barbecue sauce on one slice of the bread.
